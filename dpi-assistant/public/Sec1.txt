Education
Allow students to link all educational and skilling records through a unified Student ID
BB: Functional IDs

Students collect multiple academic, vocational, and extracurricular records across different institutions. A verifiable Student ID would let them link all certificates to one trusted source, making it easy to share with universities or employers. It would also help governments track who has already received scholarships or training benefits, preventing duplication of resources.

Allow students to auto-apply for eligible scholarships and education-assistance programs
 BB: Registries

Student ID databases can be connected to trusted institutions to match students with scholarships based on criteria like income level or GPA. Instead of exposing personal data, the system can give a simple “yes/no” match, auto-notify eligible students, and let them consent to apply—ensuring deserving students don’t miss out.

Allow students and teachers to prove their credentials in a digitally verifiable manner
 BB: Data Sharing

Certificates and marksheets are often forged, creating low trust for employers or foreign institutions. Digitally signed records, protected by cryptography, ensure tamper-proof sharing and allow any third party to verify authenticity without needing the issuing authority.
